Dr. Shabi is a senior research scientist in the Department of Plant Pathology at the ARO. He received his B.S. and M.S. in agronomy in 1960 and his Ph.D. in plant pathology from the Hebrew University of Jerusalem in 1979. He joined the ARO in 1966 as a researcher focused on deciduous fruit, grape, and olive diseases. Dr. Shabi has spent sabbatical leaves at various institutions, including the University of Missouri, Columbia (1974-1975), the University of California, Davis, Cornell University (NYSAES, Geneva, NY, 1980), INRA, France, Lincoln University, New Zealand, and Clemson University, SC (1991-1992), and HRI, Wellesbourne, UK (1996-1997). His research has centered on disease control, epidemiology, and resistance of fungi to fungicides.
